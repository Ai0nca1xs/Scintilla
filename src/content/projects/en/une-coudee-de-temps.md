"Une Coudée de Temps" (A Cubit of Time) captures the intangible presence of
time by combining light and matter, juxtaposing the ephemeral and the eternal.
In this installation, photosensitive resin records the variations of the
environment, while acrylic and glass pigments reveal the strata of time, and
metal brings stability and strength. The work is not limited to a material
manifestation: it proposes a reflection on memory, perception and the flow of
time.

By adjusting the focal distance of the lenses attached to the metal rods with
a push-and-pull gesture, the installation generates infinite variations in the
projection cast on the wall.

In this post-pandemic era, where social bonds are fractured and individual
life becomes central, my light installations seek to explore the connection
between the environment and the individual through variations and intensities
of light. Playing with light and shadow, these works reflect on how we find a
balance between the past and an uncertain future, while carrying a message of
hope and resilience in the face of hardship.
