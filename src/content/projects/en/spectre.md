Spectre is an exploration of the traces left by absence — of the fragile
connections that resist erasure, bearing witness to a humanity that keeps
vibrating even in the shadows. Captured in China during the pandemic years,
these photographs reflect a time marked by uncertainty, when every gaze, every
gesture, every interaction carried the weight of a world in transition.

In a world where distance was imposed, I sought to reveal what persisted: the
light in the eyes, the warmth of an exchange, the quiet resilience of everyday
life. These are not merely snapshots of urban life, but the testimony of a
humanity reinventing itself — finding, despite erasure and fragmentation, new
ways of existing and of being bound to one another.
